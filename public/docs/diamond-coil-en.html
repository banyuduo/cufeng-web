Industry Pain Points: Thermal Bottlenecks at High Frequency & Power

ACR Surge

At 85kHz–150kHz, skin and proximity effects reduce effective conductive cross-section; Joule heating rises nonlinearly with power.

Limited Traditional Heat Path

Litz wire + thermal paste + cold plate structures require heat to pass through multiple insulating layers, causing severe thermal resistance and hotspots.

Physical Limits of Existing Liquid Cooling

Why traditional thermal epoxy (1–3 W/m·K) no longer meets requirements

P1

Insulation Barrier

Low-thermal-conductivity polymer enamel blocks heat from escaping the copper core.

P2

Radial Obstruction

Micro-gaps and enamel contacts in stranded wire reduce effective radial thermal conductivity.

P3

Interface Contact Failure

Lack of atomic-level bonding between conductor and potting creates contact thermal resistance.
