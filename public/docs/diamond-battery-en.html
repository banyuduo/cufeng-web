Industry Background & Technical Challenges

All-solid-state batteries (ASSBs) are seen as a key technology to break through the energy density ceiling of current lithium-ion batteries. However, in the transition from lab to scale, the industry faces dual bottlenecks at both the "material" and "structural" levels.

🛑 Dendrite Penetration & Short Circuit

During charging, needle-like dendrites easily form on the lithium metal anode surface. Existing carbon host materials typically have modulus < 10 GPa, unable to physically block dendrite growth, leading to electrolyte penetration and short circuit.

🛑 Interface Contact Failure

Electrode materials undergo large volume changes (breathing effect) during charge/discharge. Traditional physically stacked solid-solid interfaces are prone to delamination, causing interface impedance (Rct) to rise sharply.

🛑 Complex Manufacturing

Cathode, electrolyte, and anode are typically prepared separately and then assembled; the process chain is long, and scaling ultrathin electrolyte layers is difficult.

Base Technology: High-Modulus Anode Host Material

Product A: Diamond/Carbon Composite Anode Host

Core Material

Addressing the pain points of "dendrite penetration" and "volume expansion" in lithium metal anodes, we developed a 3D skeleton material based on sp³-sp² covalent bonding network. This forms the foundation of our entire technology system.

💎 High Modulus (>100 GPa)

Nano-diamond is introduced as rigid "rivets" to build a physical "dendrite exclusion zone," effectively mitigating short-circuit risks.

🔋 Zero-Strain Lithium Storage

Rich internal connected pores accommodate metal lithium deposition, achieving near-zero volume expansion during charge/discharge.

⚡ Gradient Lithiophilic Design

In-situ doping (B/N/Si) induces lithium ions to preferentially deposit inside the skeleton, achieving uniform dendrite-free lithium plating.

Advanced Technology: Integrated Gradient Skeleton Structure

Product B

Monolithic Integrated Skeleton

Building on Product A's material advantages, we further propose an innovative "zoned functionalization + monolithic co-sintering" architecture, fusing cathode, electrolyte, and anode matrix into a single rigid block.

  • Seamless interface: Cathode, electrolyte, and anode skeletons form an atomically bonded continuous whole through co-sintering, effectively eliminating physical seams; interface impedance approaches liquid battery levels.
  • Pore gradient design (see right):
    - Electrode zones: micron-scale macropores for high-capacity active material loading.
    - Electrolyte zone: nanoscale mesopores for electrolyte self-adsorption and locking via capillary pressure difference; physically blocks cathode-anode interpenetration.
  • Triple insulation strategy: Insulation coating or graded composition achieves electronic insulation while maintaining mechanical continuity.

Potential Application Advantages & Value

📱 Consumer Electronics (3C)

Eliminate "swelling" anxiety: Zero-strain skeleton enables device makers to avoid reserving physical space for battery expansion, significantly increasing capacity while keeping thickness unchanged.
Better fast-charging: sp² conductive skeleton provides excellent electron transport channels, supporting high-rate fast charging without overheating.

🚁 Low-Altitude Economy (UAV/eVTOL)

High energy density: Enables lithium metal anode, significantly increasing gravimetric energy density (Wh/kg), directly translating to longer flight time and range.
Structural safety: The monolithic rigid skeleton itself serves as a structural component; maintains cell integrity under drop or impact, mitigating fire/explosion risk.

🚗 New Energy Vehicles (EV)

Simplified pack process: High mechanical strength of single cells allows simplified external pressure structure for Battery Pack, improving GCTP integration efficiency.
Manufacturing cost reduction: Supports "ingot sintering + wire cutting" process similar to PV industry; potential to significantly reduce solid-state battery manufacturing cost.

🔋 Grid-Scale Storage

Long cycle life: Rigid skeleton limits mechanical pulverization of electrode materials during repeated charge/discharge; theoretical cycle life far exceeds traditional lithium-ion batteries.
Inherent safety: No flammable liquid components; effectively addresses thermal runaway risk in energy storage stations.
